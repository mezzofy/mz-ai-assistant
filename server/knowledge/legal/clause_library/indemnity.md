# Indemnity Clauses — Standard Library

## 1. MUTUAL INDEMNITY (Standard)
Each Party ("Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other Party and its officers, directors, employees, and agents ("Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all claims, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising from or relating to:
(a) any breach of this Agreement by the Indemnifying Party;
(b) any negligent or wilful act or omission of the Indemnifying Party; or
(c) any infringement of third-party intellectual property rights by the Indemnifying Party.

## 2. IP INFRINGEMENT INDEMNITY (Vendor-side)
The Vendor shall indemnify the Client against all losses arising from any claim that the Deliverables infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party, provided that:
(a) the Client promptly notifies the Vendor of any such claim;
(b) the Vendor has sole control of the defence and settlement; and
(c) the Client provides reasonable assistance in the defence.

## 3. DATA BREACH INDEMNITY
The Data Processor shall indemnify the Data Controller for all costs, fines, penalties, and damages resulting from any data breach caused by the Data Processor's failure to comply with its obligations under this Agreement or applicable data protection laws (including PDPA / GDPR / UAE PDPL as applicable).

## 4. CAPPED INDEMNITY
The total aggregate liability of each Party under the indemnities in this Agreement shall not exceed [AMOUNT / a multiple of the contract value, e.g., 2× annual fees].

## 5. EXCLUDED MATTERS
No indemnity shall apply to claims arising from:
(a) the Indemnified Party's own negligence or wilful misconduct;
(b) modifications to the Deliverables made without the Indemnifying Party's consent;
(c) use of the Deliverables other than as intended or specified.

## Notes for Leo
- Singapore: indemnity clauses generally enforceable; courts enforce clear and unambiguous language
- HK: common law indemnity principles apply; contra proferentem rule for ambiguities
- UAE (onshore): Civil Code allows indemnity; must not exclude liability for gross negligence or wilful act
- Cayman: common law indemnity; director indemnification in Articles of Association is standard for exempted companies
