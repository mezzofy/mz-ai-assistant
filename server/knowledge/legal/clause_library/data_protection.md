# Data Protection Clauses — Standard Library

## 1. GENERAL DATA PROTECTION COMPLIANCE
Each Party shall comply with all applicable data protection and privacy laws in the performance of this Agreement, including (as applicable): Singapore PDPA 2012, Hong Kong PDPO (Cap. 486), Malaysia PDPA 2010, UAE PDPL (Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021), DIFC Data Protection Law 2020, Saudi Arabia PDPL, Qatar Personal Data Privacy Law, Cayman Islands Data Protection Act 2021, and/or the EU GDPR.

## 2. DATA PROCESSING OBLIGATIONS (Processor)
The Data Processor (Service Provider) shall:
(a) process personal data only on documented instructions from the Data Controller (Client);
(b) implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or destruction;
(c) not engage any sub-processor without the Data Controller's prior written consent;
(d) notify the Data Controller within [48 / 72] hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach;
(e) assist the Data Controller in responding to data subject requests;
(f) return or delete all personal data upon termination of this Agreement;
(g) make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this clause.

## 3. CROSS-BORDER DATA TRANSFER
Neither Party shall transfer personal data outside [SPECIFIED JURISDICTION(S)] without:
(a) ensuring the recipient jurisdiction provides an adequate level of data protection; or
(b) implementing appropriate safeguards (such as standard contractual clauses or binding corporate rules); or
(c) obtaining explicit consent from the data subjects.

## 4. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION
In the event of a personal data breach, the Party experiencing the breach shall:
(a) notify the other Party within [24 / 48] hours;
(b) notify the competent data protection authority within the timeframe required by applicable law (e.g., 3 business days under Singapore PDPA; 72 hours under UAE PDPL and GDPR);
(c) take immediate steps to contain and remediate the breach;
(d) cooperate fully in any investigation.

## Notes for Leo
- Singapore PDPA: notification within 3 business days to PDPC if breach likely to cause significant harm or >500 individuals affected
- HK PDPO: no mandatory breach notification in the ordinance (PCPD recommends prompt notification)
- Malaysia PDPA: no mandatory breach notification currently; best practice to notify
- UAE PDPL: 72 hours notification to UAE Data Office where breach may harm data subject
- Saudi PDPL: 72 hours to SDAIA
- DIFC: notification within 72 hours to Commissioner of Data Protection
