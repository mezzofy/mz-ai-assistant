# Intellectual Property Clauses — Standard Library

## 1. CLIENT OWNS ALL DELIVERABLES (Work-for-Hire)
All intellectual property rights (including copyright, patents, trade marks, database rights, and all other IP) in and to the Deliverables and any other materials created by the Service Provider specifically for the Client under this Agreement shall vest in and be owned by the Client upon creation (or upon full payment, if payment has not been made). The Service Provider assigns all such rights to the Client with full title guarantee.

## 2. SERVICE PROVIDER RETAINS BACKGROUND IP
The Client acknowledges that the Service Provider may use its own pre-existing tools, methodologies, code libraries, and know-how ("Background IP") in performing the Services. The Service Provider retains all rights in its Background IP. To the extent Background IP is incorporated in the Deliverables, the Service Provider grants the Client a non-exclusive, irrevocable, royalty-free licence to use such Background IP solely as part of the Deliverables.

## 3. MUTUAL IP — JOINT DEVELOPMENT
Intellectual property jointly created by both Parties during the collaboration ("Joint IP") shall be jointly owned. Each Party may use Joint IP without accounting to the other, unless agreed otherwise. Neither Party may assign or licence Joint IP to a third party without the other Party's written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld).

## 4. EMPLOYEE IP ASSIGNMENT (Employment Context)
The Employee acknowledges that all inventions, discoveries, software, and works of authorship made by the Employee in the course of employment or using Company resources shall automatically vest in and be owned by the Company ("Company IP"). The Employee shall promptly disclose all Company IP to the Company and execute all documents necessary to perfect the Company's title.

## 5. IP INDEMNITY
The Party providing materials warrants that they do not infringe any third-party IP rights and shall indemnify the other Party against any claims arising from such infringement.

## Notes for Leo
- Singapore: Copyright Act 2021 — employer owns work created by employee in course of employment (s.131)
  Patents Act — employee inventions: if made in normal course of duties → employer; if outside → employee
- HK: Copyright Ordinance — employer first owner of work made by employee in employment; commissioned works: author first owner unless agreed otherwise
- Malaysia: Copyright Act 1987 — employer first owner of work made in employment; commissioned works: commissioning party owns (unlike HK)
- UAE (DIFC): common law principles — employee vs. consultant distinction matters
- UAE (onshore): Federal IP law — employer typically owns work-for-hire; consultation with counsel recommended
