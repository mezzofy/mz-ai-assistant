# Governing Law Clauses — Standard Library

## 1. SINGAPORE LAW
This Agreement and any dispute or claim (including non-contractual disputes or claims) arising out of or in connection with it or its subject matter or formation shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the law of the Republic of Singapore.

## 2. HONG KONG LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China.

## 3. ENGLISH LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales.

## 4. UAE LAW (ONSHORE)
This Agreement shall be governed by the federal laws of the United Arab Emirates and, to the extent applicable, the laws of the Emirate of [Dubai / Abu Dhabi / applicable emirate].

## 5. DIFC LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Dubai International Financial Centre ("DIFC").

## 6. CAYMAN ISLANDS LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Cayman Islands.

## 7. CHOICE OF LAW — CROSS-BORDER (MULTI-JURISDICTION OPTION)
This Agreement shall be governed by [GOVERNING LAW]. The Parties have chosen this governing law as a neutral jurisdiction mutually acceptable to both Parties.

## Notes for Leo
- Choice of governing law is generally respected by courts in SG, HK, UAE (DIFC), Cayman Islands under common law
- UAE (onshore): choice of foreign law is respected in contract but may be overridden by UAE mandatory provisions
- Saudi Arabia: Saudi law and Sharia always apply to activities within KSA regardless of choice of law clause
- Malaysia: choice of law generally respected; Malaysia Contracts Act 1950 may impose mandatory requirements
- Always pair governing law with a dispute resolution clause (arbitration or courts) specifying the seat
