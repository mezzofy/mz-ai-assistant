# Confidentiality Clauses — Standard Library

## 1. STANDARD CONFIDENTIALITY (Long Form)
Each Party ("Receiving Party") shall keep strictly confidential all non-public information and materials of the other Party ("Disclosing Party") disclosed in connection with this Agreement ("Confidential Information"), and shall:
(a) not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing its obligations under this Agreement;
(b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;
(c) protect Confidential Information using at least the same measures as it uses for its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care;
(d) limit access to Confidential Information to those employees and advisers who need to know and are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

## 2. EXCEPTIONS TO CONFIDENTIALITY
Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement;
(b) was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure (as evidenced by written records);
(c) is lawfully received from a third party without restriction;
(d) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information; or
(e) is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority (provided prompt notice is given and disclosure is limited to what is required).

## 3. SHORT-FORM CONFIDENTIALITY (Inline for Contracts)
Each Party shall treat all non-public information received from the other Party as strictly confidential and shall not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party. This obligation shall survive termination of this Agreement for [3] years.

## 4. PERPETUAL CONFIDENTIALITY (Trade Secrets)
Notwithstanding any other provision, the Receiving Party's obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue indefinitely for as long as the information remains a trade secret.

## 5. EMPLOYEE / CONTRACTOR CONFIDENTIALITY
The Employer/Company reserves the right to monitor access to Confidential Information. The Employee/Contractor acknowledges that breach of this clause entitles the Employer/Company to seek injunctive relief without proof of actual damage.

## Notes for Leo
- Singapore: NDA enforceable; breach of confidence action available in equity; injunctions commonly granted
- HK: equitable duty of confidence (Coco v Clark); springboard doctrine extends post-employment obligations
- Malaysia: equitable breach of confidence; courts apply English law principles; trade secrets regime developing
- UAE: Law No. 36 of 2021 (IP law) protects trade secrets; DIFC/ADGM — common law breach of confidence
- Saudi Arabia: SAIP Trade Secret Regulations (2020) + contractual NDA; Saudi courts recognise confidentiality obligations
- Qatar: contractual protection primary; PDPL adds layer for personal data confidentiality
