# Limitation of Liability Clauses — Standard Library

## 1. AGGREGATE CAP (Standard)
Each Party's total aggregate liability to the other Party under or in connection with this Agreement, whether arising in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty, or otherwise, shall not exceed [AMOUNT / the total fees paid or payable in the [12] months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim].

## 2. CONSEQUENTIAL LOSS EXCLUSION (Standard)
Neither Party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, consequential, incidental, special, punitive, or exemplary loss or damage, including:
(a) loss of profits or revenue;
(b) loss of business or contracts;
(c) loss of anticipated savings;
(d) loss of goodwill or reputation;
(e) loss of data,
in each case, whether or not such losses were foreseeable or the Party had been advised of the possibility of such losses.

## 3. CARVE-OUTS (Exceptions — Cannot Be Limited)
Nothing in this Agreement limits liability:
(a) for death or personal injury caused by negligence;
(b) for fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
(c) for any other liability that cannot be excluded or limited by applicable law.
[Add as required: (d) for breach of data protection obligations; (e) for wilful misconduct.]

## 4. MUTUAL CAP WITH MINIMUM
Notwithstanding the cap in Clause [X], each Party's liability shall not be less than [MINIMUM AMOUNT, e.g., SGD 10,000 / USD 10,000].

## Notes for Leo
- Singapore: UCTA (Unfair Contract Terms Act) applies to standard-form contracts — caps must be "reasonable"
  Reasonableness factors: relative bargaining power, industry practice, availability of insurance
- HK: no UCTA equivalent — contractual caps more freely enforceable
- UAE (onshore): Civil Code — exclusion of liability for gross negligence or wilful misconduct is void
- Malaysia: Contracts Act 1950 — exclusion of liability for fraud or negligence causing death/injury void
- Cayman: common law — caps enforceable; sophisticated parties standard applies
