# Termination Clauses — Standard Library

## 1. TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE
Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time for any reason by giving [30 / 60 / 90] days' prior written notice to the other Party. Upon termination, all accrued but unpaid obligations shall be honoured; no further liability arises.

## 2. TERMINATION FOR CAUSE (Material Breach)
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately (or after a cure period) if:
(a) the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and (if the breach is capable of remedy) fails to remedy the breach within [14 / 30] days of receiving written notice specifying the breach; or
(b) the other Party becomes insolvent, goes into administration, or makes an arrangement with creditors.

## 3. TERMINATION FOR INSOLVENCY
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
(a) the other Party is unable to pay its debts as they fall due;
(b) a receiver, administrator, or liquidator is appointed over any material part of its assets;
(c) the other Party passes a resolution for winding up (other than for solvent reconstruction).

## 4. EFFECTS OF TERMINATION
Upon termination of this Agreement:
(a) all licences granted hereunder terminate immediately;
(b) each Party shall return or destroy the other's confidential information;
(c) accrued rights and obligations are not affected;
(d) Clauses [LIST SURVIVING CLAUSES — e.g., Confidentiality, IP, Liability, Governing Law] shall survive;
(e) the Service Provider shall deliver all work in progress to the Client.

## 5. PAYMENT ON TERMINATION
On termination, the Client shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed up to the termination date at the agreed rate. If terminated for cause by the Client, the Service Provider shall refund any advance payments for Services not performed.

## Notes for Leo
- Singapore: Employment Act sets minimum notice periods — cannot be contracted out below statutory minimums
- HK: Employment Ordinance sets minimum notice (1 month); summary dismissal only for serious misconduct
- Malaysia: Employment Act notice periods apply; Industrial Court reinstatement remedy available for wrongful dismissal
- UAE Labour Law (2021): fixed-term contracts maximum 3 years; termination without cause requires notice (30 days min) + end-of-service gratuity
- Saudi Arabia: Labour Law — termination for cause (Article 80 violations); without cause requires notice + severance
