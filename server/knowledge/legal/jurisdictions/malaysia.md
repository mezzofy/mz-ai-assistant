# Malaysia Business Law — Reference Guide

## Legal System
Common law jurisdiction (based on English common law), with Syariah law applying to personal
and family matters for Muslims. Federal constitution is the supreme law.
Primary courts: Magistrates' Court, Sessions Court, High Court (Malaya / Sabah & Sarawak),
Court of Appeal, Federal Court (highest). Syariah courts are separate and parallel.
AIAC (Asian International Arbitration Centre, formerly KLRCA) is the primary arbitration centre.

## Key Statutes for Business

### Companies Act 2016
- Replaced Companies Act 1965 — major reform
- Sendirian Berhad (Sdn Bhd) — private limited company, most common for foreign investors
- Berhad (Bhd) — public company
- Minimum 1 director, one of whom must ordinarily reside in Malaysia
- Annual returns filed with SSM (Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia — Companies Commission)
- Audit: required for Sdn Bhd companies except for dormant companies
- No minimum paid-up capital requirement (RM 1 minimum)
- Company Secretary: mandatory; must be licensed by SSM or member of prescribed bodies

### Employment Act 1955 (as amended by Act A1651, effective 2023)
- Covers employees earning ≤ RM 4,000/month for Part-time Employment provisions
  (expanded in 2023 amendments — verify current threshold)
- 2023 amendments: flexible work arrangements (employees can request, employer must respond within 60 days)
- Notice of termination: depends on contract; Employment Act minimums:
  <2 years: 4 weeks; 2–5 years: 6 weeks; >5 years: 8 weeks
- Annual leave: 8 days (<2 years); 12 days (2–5 years); 16 days (>5 years)
- Sick leave: 14 days (<2 years); 18 days (2–5 years); 22 days (>5 years)
- Maternity leave: 98 days (extended from 60 days under 2023 amendments)
- Paternity leave: 7 days
- EPF (Employees Provident Fund): 12% employer contribution, 11% employee (age ≤ 60)
- SOCSO and EIS contributions also required
- Termination: Industrial Court adjudicates wrongful dismissal — reinstatement possible

### Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)
- Governs processing of personal data in commercial transactions
- Seven Data Protection Principles
- Personal Data Protection Commissioner is the regulator
- Certain sectors exempt: federal/state governments, domestic use
- Data transfer to foreign countries permitted only to approved countries (list gazetted)
- No mandatory breach notification requirement under PDPA (but best practice)
- Penalties: fines up to RM 500,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 years

### Contracts Act 1950
- Based on Indian Contracts Act — codified common law principles
- Written contracts recommended but oral contracts generally enforceable
- Specific Performance: equitable remedy available for breach of land-related contracts
- Misrepresentation: statutory remedies under Contracts Act and Specific Relief Act 1950
- Restraint of trade clauses: void under s.28 unless reasonable — SG/HK standard does not apply
  (Malaysia courts apply stricter void-unless-exception rule — clauses need careful drafting)

### Intellectual Property Laws
- Trade Marks Act 2019: modernised ASEAN-aligned registration system; Madrid Protocol accession
- Patents Act 1983: 20-year protection; MYIPO examination; divisional patents recognised
- Copyright Act 1987: automatic protection; life of author + 50 years for literary works
- Industrial Designs Act 1996
- MYIPO (Malaysia Intellectual Property Corporation): registration body

### Arbitration Act 2005
- Based on UNCITRAL Model Law
- AIAC (Kuala Lumpur): well-established regional arbitration centre
- Domestic and international arbitration distinguished
- Malaysian courts generally uphold arbitration agreements and awards
- Foreign arbitral awards enforceable under Arbitration Act 2005 (New York Convention)

## Standard Contractual Practices
- Contracts in Bahasa Malaysia (Malay) or English; both legally valid
- Stamp Duty Act 1949: stamp duty on agreements, transfers, and securities
- Limitation period: 6 years (Limitation Act 1953)
- Electronic signatures: Electronic Commerce Act 2006 — valid for most commercial transactions
- Courts apply contractual interpretation principles similar to English common law

## Standard NDA / Confidentiality Clause Notes
- NDA enforceable with valid consideration
- Non-compete clauses: s.28 Contracts Act — restraint of trade clauses are prima facie void
  Exception: enforceable if protecting legitimate proprietary interests (client lists, trade secrets)
  Duration must be reasonable; geographic scope must be limited
- Injunctions available for breach of confidentiality
- Trade secret protection: primarily through contract law and equitable breach of confidence action
  (no dedicated trade secrets legislation as of 2026)

## Employment Contract Requirements
- Employment Act employees: written contract recommended; statutory rights apply regardless
- Termination: must have just cause and excuse (per Employment Act and Industrial Relations Act 1967)
- Fixed-term contracts: courts may imply indefinite tenure if renewals are habitual
- Probation: typically 3–6 months; termination during probation still requires just cause
- Foreign workers: Employment Pass or Professional Visit Pass required

## Common Risk Areas for Foreign Companies
- Bumiputera requirements: certain sectors require Bumiputera equity participation (30% minimum in some industries)
- FDI restrictions: Malaysia Investment Development Authority (MIDA) approval required for certain manufacturing
- MSC Malaysia status: ICT companies can apply for tax incentives and foreign-worker flexibility
- Sdn Bhd vs. branch office: Sdn Bhd preferred; branch has unlimited HQ liability
- Labour disputes: Industrial Court is employee-friendly; wrongful dismissal claims are common
- Tax: corporate tax at 24% (SMEs: 17% on first RM 600,000); withholding tax on royalties/services
- GST (reintroduced discussions ongoing — verify current status as of implementation date)
- Halal compliance: consumer products, F&B, pharmaceuticals may require JAKIM halal certification
- Anti-corruption: MACC Act 2009 — corporate liability for bribery by associated persons (commercial organisations)
- Labuan offshore structures: available for international business and holding structures at 3% tax
