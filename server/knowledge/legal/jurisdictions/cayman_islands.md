# Cayman Islands Business Law — Reference Guide

## Legal System
British Overseas Territory. Common law jurisdiction based on English common law principles,
with significant modifications under Cayman Islands statutes.
Cayman Islands Grand Court: the principal court; experienced in complex commercial, insolvency, and fund disputes.
Cayman Islands Court of Appeal; Privy Council (London) is the final appellate court.

The Cayman Islands is the world's leading offshore fund domicile and a top-5 global financial centre.
Reasons for popularity:
1. Zero corporate tax, capital gains tax, withholding tax, or inheritance tax
2. Flexible company structures (especially Exempted Company and ELP)
3. Recognised by institutional investors globally
4. CIMA (Cayman Islands Monetary Authority): well-regarded financial regulator
5. Strong legal infrastructure; sophisticated Grand Court familiar with complex fund structures

Common use cases:
- Offshore holding company structures
- Venture capital and private equity fund vehicles
- Hedge fund domicile
- IP holding structures
- SPV for cross-border M&A transactions
- Cayman SPC (Segregated Portfolio Company) for multi-class investment structures

## Key Statutes for Business

### Companies Act (2023 Revision)
- Governs all companies incorporated in Cayman Islands
- **Exempted Company** (most common for offshore holding and funds):
  - Cannot trade within Cayman Islands (must carry on business "outside" Cayman)
  - No Cayman resident director required
  - No public disclosure of shareholders or financial statements
  - Minimum 1 director; corporate directors permitted (unusual flexibility)
  - Registered office in Cayman required (registered agent required)
  - Annual government renewal fee based on authorised capital
  - No audit requirement unless imposed by CIMA (for regulated entities)
- **Ordinary Resident Company** (for local business): subject to more requirements
- **Unlimited Liability Company (ULC)**: used in specific structures

### Exempted Limited Partnership Act (2021 Revision)
- **Exempted Limited Partnership (ELP)**: the standard vehicle for private equity and venture capital funds
- Must have at least 1 general partner (GP) and 1 limited partner (LP)
- GP has unlimited liability; LPs have limited liability (cannot participate in management)
- GP can be any entity (Cayman or foreign); common to use Cayman Exempted Company as GP
- No public disclosure of LP identities
- No requirement to file accounts with government
- CIMA registration required if carrying on "fund business" (as defined under Private Funds Act)

### Limited Liability Companies Act (2021 Revision) — Cayman LLC
- Cayman LLC structure (modelled on Delaware LLC)
- Flexibility of members' agreement; no separate GP/LP distinction
- Useful for joint ventures and club deals
- Members have limited liability
- Can be registered as a fund vehicle

### Private Funds Act (2020) and Mutual Funds Act (2021 Revision)
- **Private Funds Act**: applies to Cayman-registered vehicles (ELP, company, LLC) that are "private funds"
  i.e., closed-end funds with 2 or more investors; must register with CIMA
  Minimum requirements: auditor (CIMA-approved), AML policies, valuation policies, custody arrangements
- **Mutual Funds Act**: applies to open-ended funds (hedge funds); must register or be licensed with CIMA
  Administered and managed funds must be licensed; self-managed funds may qualify for exemptions
- Cayman Islands: requires filing of annual accounts with CIMA for registered funds

### Anti-Money Laundering Regulations (2024 Revision)
- All Cayman entities must comply with AML/CFT requirements
- CIMA oversight for regulated entities; financial institutions must have AML compliance programmes
- Beneficial ownership: Cayman entities must maintain a Register of Members/Partners; beneficial ownership information submitted to and held by registered agents and accessible to Cayman authorities
- FATCA and CRS: automatic exchange of financial account information with treaty partners (US FATCA, OECD CRS)

### Securities Investment Business Act (SIBA) (2021 Revision)
- Governs investment business activities in Cayman
- CIMA regulates: securities investment business, securities arrangements, mutual fund administration
- Registered person vs. licensed person: regulatory requirements differ

### Economic Substance Act (2020 Revision)
- Cayman entities conducting "relevant activities" (banking, insurance, fund management, finance, HQ, IP, distribution) must demonstrate economic substance in Cayman Islands
- Substance requirements: adequate staff, premises, and expenditure in Cayman
- Pure equity holding companies: simplified substance requirements
- Non-compliance: penalties including striking off

## Standard Contractual Practices
- Contracts in English (official language)
- No stamp duty on contracts (no stamp duty on instruments generally)
- Limitation periods: Limitation Law (2020 Revision) — 6 years for simple contracts; 12 years for deeds
- Electronic signatures: Electronic Transactions Act (2021 Revision) — valid for commercial transactions
- No capital controls; free flow of funds in and out of Cayman Islands

## Standard NDA / Confidentiality Clause Notes
- NDA enforceable under Cayman common law (based on English law principles)
- Cayman courts apply English law precedents including Saltman Engineering test for confidentiality
- Duration: perpetual or time-limited — both enforceable
- Non-compete: enforceable if reasonable; assessed under English common law reasonableness test
- Injunctions: readily available from Cayman Grand Court for urgent confidentiality breaches

## Fund Documents (Specific to Cayman Fund Structures)
- **Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA)**: the constitutional document for ELPs
  Must specify: capital commitment, draw-down mechanics, GP/LP rights, distribution waterfall (preferred return, carried interest), transfer restrictions, key-man, no-fault removal, LPAC (Limited Partner Advisory Committee)
- **Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)**: offering document for fund investors; must disclose material risks
- **Subscription Agreement**: investor onboarding; representations and warranties; AML/KYC
- **Side Letters**: LP-specific preferential terms; most favoured nation clauses
- **Management Agreement**: GP or fund manager engagement terms
- **Carried Interest**: typical PE structure: 8% preferred return, 20% carry (2%/20% hedge fund)

## Shareholder / Members Agreement Key Clauses (Exempted Company)
- Reserved matters: board veto, shareholder veto for material transactions
- Pre-emption rights: right of first refusal on share transfers
- Anti-dilution: weighted average or full-ratchet protection for preferred shareholders
- Drag-along: majority can force minority to sell
- Tag-along: minority can join majority sale on same terms
- Board composition: investor director appointment rights
- Information rights: quarterly financial reporting, access to accounts
- IPO / Exit: registration rights, lock-up periods

## Common Risk Areas for Foreign Companies
- Economic substance: entities with HQ or IP holding activities must demonstrate substance — non-compliance = CIMA reporting to home jurisdiction and financial penalties
- FATCA/CRS: all financial account information automatically reported to relevant tax authorities; no Cayman Islands "secrecy" for tax purposes
- Beneficial ownership: while not publicly disclosed, beneficial ownership information held by registered agents and can be requested by authorities (law enforcement, tax authorities under exchange agreements)
- AML compliance: CIMA and registered agents are strict; inadequate AML procedures = regulatory action
- Private Funds Act compliance: funds that skip CIMA registration risk winding-up orders and CIMA action
- US persons: must exclude US investors from non-CIMA-registered structures (Rule 3(c)(1) and 3(c)(7) exemptions from US Investment Company Act); legal counsel required
- PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company): Cayman funds investing in US assets may trigger US tax issues for US investors — tax counsel required
- Cayman restructuring: Grand Court has efficient scheme of arrangement and provisional liquidation procedures for distressed restructuring
