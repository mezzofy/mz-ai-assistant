# Hong Kong Business Law — Reference Guide

## Legal System
Common law jurisdiction, maintained post-1997 under the Basic Law ("one country, two systems").
English law principles apply with Hong Kong Ordinance modifications.
Primary courts: Magistrates' Courts, District Court, Court of First Instance (CFI),
Court of Appeal, Court of Final Appeal (CFA) — highest court in HK.
HKIAC is the primary arbitration centre; Hong Kong is a Tier 1 global arbitration seat.

## Key Statutes for Business

### Companies Ordinance (Cap. 622)
- Governs incorporation, management, and winding up of companies
- Private limited company (Ltd.) — most common structure for foreign investment
- Minimum 1 director; no residency requirement for directors
- Annual return filed with Companies Registry (CR)
- AGM: private companies exempt unless required by constitution
- Share capital: no minimum share capital requirement
- Audit: required for all companies (exemption for small private companies since 2015)

### Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57)
- Covers employees under continuous employment (4+ weeks, 18+ hours/week)
- Notice of termination: 1 month minimum (or payment in lieu) unless shorter period agreed
- Severance payment: 2/3 × last month's wages × years of service (continuous employment >24 months)
- Long service payment: 2/3 × last month's wages × years of service (>5 years, not entitled to severance)
- Statutory holidays: 12 days/year; Annual leave: 7–14 days depending on service length
- Sick leave: 2 sickness days/month up to 120 days accumulated (paid at 4/5 of daily wage)
- Maternity leave: 14 weeks (paid); Paternity leave: 5 days (paid)
- Mandatory Provident Fund (MPF): 5% employer + 5% employee contribution (cap HKD 1,500/month each)

### Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (Cap. 486) — PDPO
- Six Data Protection Principles govern collection, use, retention, security, and access
- Privacy Commissioner for Personal Data (PCPD) is the regulator
- No mandatory breach notification requirement in the ordinance (but PCPD guidance recommends prompt notification)
- Data retention: retain only as long as necessary for the purpose
- Transfers to jurisdictions without equivalent protection require contractual safeguards
- Penalty for criminal offences: HKD 50,000 fine and 2 years imprisonment

### Securities and Futures Ordinance (Cap. 571)
- SFC is the primary securities regulator
- Type 1–10 licences for regulated activities (dealing, advising, managing, clearing, etc.)
- SFC Codes of Conduct for licensed firms
- Insider dealing and market manipulation: criminal offences with up to 10 years imprisonment
- Securities margin lending: regulated by HK Monetary Authority (HKMA)

### Arbitration Ordinance (Cap. 609)
- Based on UNCITRAL Model Law (2006 version)
- HKIAC — Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre — globally recognised
- HK courts are pro-arbitration; non-intervention principle strictly applied
- Interim measures: HK courts can grant interim relief in support of arbitration
- New York Convention: HK arbitral awards enforceable in 160+ countries

### Intellectual Property
- Trade Marks Ordinance (Cap. 559): registration with IPD (Intellectual Property Department)
- Patents Ordinance (Cap. 514): re-registration of PRC, UK, or EP(UK) patents; short-term patents
- Copyright Ordinance (Cap. 528): automatic protection; 50 years from creation (author's life + 50 for literary works)
- Employer owns IP created in course of employment unless agreed otherwise

## Standard Contractual Practices
- Contracts in English; Traditional Chinese also used
- Electronic signatures: Electronic Transactions Ordinance (Cap. 553) — recognition for most transactions
- Stamp Duty: payable on share transfers (0.26% of consideration), property transactions
- Limitation period: 6 years for simple contracts; 12 years for contracts under seal
- Implied terms: Sale of Goods Ordinance, Supply of Services implied terms
- No Unfair Contract Terms legislation equivalent to UK — limited protection for consumers

## Standard NDA / Confidentiality Clause Notes
- NDA enforceable; must have valid consideration
- Duration: typically 2–5 years; perpetual NDAs valid but harder to enforce indefinitely
- Non-compete covenants: must be reasonable — HK courts apply "reasonableness" test strictly
- Injunctions: available and frequently granted for breach of confidentiality in employment context
- Spring-board doctrine: ex-employee cannot use confidential information even after it becomes public

## Employment Contract Requirements
- No statutory requirement for written contract but Employment Ordinance rights apply regardless
- Key terms should be documented: job title, hours, wage, leave, probation period
- Probation: typically 1–3 months; no statutory maximum
- Immediate termination: only for serious misconduct (summary dismissal)
- Garden leave: enforceable with clear contractual basis

## Common Risk Areas for Foreign Companies
- No MPF exemption for directors — contribution required for all covered employees
- Tax: profits tax at 16.5% (first HKD 2M at 8.25% for corporations); salaries tax applies to HK-sourced income
- Business Registration: all businesses must register with IRD within 1 month of commencement
- Work visa (Employment Visa / IDAS): required for non-HKSAR/BN(O) employees
- Data localisation: no general requirement but financial sector has HKMA-specific rules
- Anti-money laundering: Companies Ordinance requires registered beneficial ownership records
- Connected party disclosures: listed companies have significant ongoing obligations
- Mainland China considerations: CEPA arrangements; dual-filing considerations for some regulatory activities
- Short-term patent risk: SPT grants may be challenged if claims are broad without substantive examination
