# United Arab Emirates (UAE) Business Law — Reference Guide

## Legal System
**Dual legal system — key distinction:**
1. **Mainland UAE (onshore):** Civil law (influenced by Egyptian/French civil law + Islamic Sharia principles).
   Federal laws apply across all 7 emirates. Emirate-specific laws may supplement federal law.
2. **DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre):** Common law jurisdiction with its own courts and laws.
   English common law principles apply. DIFC Courts are internationally recognised.
3. **ADGM (Abu Dhabi Global Market):** Common law jurisdiction with its own courts and laws.
   ADGM Courts apply English common law with ADGM regulations.

Primary courts:
- Federal Supreme Court (constitutional matters)
- Courts of First Instance, Appeals Courts in each emirate
- DIFC Courts (independent jurisdiction within DIFC)
- ADGM Courts (independent jurisdiction within ADGM)

**Always clarify whether the entity is onshore, DIFC, or ADGM — the applicable law differs significantly.**

## Key Statutes for Business

### Commercial Companies Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 32 of 2021)
- Major reform — 100% foreign ownership now permitted for most onshore companies (previously 51% UAE national shareholding required)
- LLC (Limited Liability Company) — most common onshore structure for foreign investors
- Minimum 2 shareholders (individuals or corporates); maximum 50 shareholders for LLC
- Branch offices: allowed for foreign companies; require commercial agent for certain activities
- Free zone companies: subject to respective free zone authority regulations (not federal Companies Law)
- Joint Stock Companies (PJSC/PSC): for larger enterprises and listed companies

### UAE Labour Law (Federal Law No. 33 of 2021)
- Applies to all private sector employees (federal and emirate level)
- Fixed-term contracts: maximum 3 years; automatically renewed if not terminated
- Notice period: minimum 30 days; maximum 90 days
- Gratuity (End of Service): 21 days' basic wage per year (first 5 years); 30 days per year thereafter (no cap effective 2022)
- Annual leave: 30 days/year (after 1 year of service)
- Sick leave: 15 days full pay, 30 days half pay, 45 days unpaid per year
- Maternity leave: 60 days (45 full pay, 15 half pay)
- Paternity leave: 5 days
- Non-compete: enforceable if restricted to specific sector, geographic area, max 2 years
- Work permits (UAE residence visa + work permit): required for all foreign employees

### Personal Data Protection Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 — PDPL)
- UAE's first comprehensive federal data protection law (effective September 2023)
- Applies to all processing of personal data in the UAE, excluding government entities
- Consent must be specific, informed, and documented
- Data subjects rights: access, correction, deletion, opt-out of direct marketing
- Data breach notification: 72 hours to UAE Data Office (where breach may harm data subject)
- Cross-border transfers: permitted only to countries with adequate protection or with safeguards
- DIFC and ADGM have their own separate data protection laws (DIFC DP Law 2020, ADGM DP Regulations)
- UAE Data Office is the federal regulator

### Civil Transactions Law (Federal Law No. 5 of 1985) — UAE Civil Code
- Governs contracts, obligations, and civil liability
- Freedom of contract principle; parties may agree terms (subject to public order and morals)
- Good faith obligation in contract performance
- Force majeure: defined in Civil Code; must be unforeseeable and unavoidable
- Interest (riba): restricted under Sharia — commercial interest permitted but not compound interest in some courts
- Assignment of contracts: requires consent of obligor (unlike common law)

### DIFC-Specific Laws (for DIFC-registered entities)
- DIFC Companies Law (DIFC Law No. 5 of 2018): common law company governance
- DIFC Employment Law: comprehensive employment protections; DIFC Courts jurisdiction
- DIFC Regulatory Framework: DFSA (Dubai Financial Services Authority) regulates financial services in DIFC
- DIFC Contract Law: based on English common law; CISG applies by reference for international sale of goods
- DIFC Insolvency Law: administration and liquidation procedures

### ADGM-Specific Laws
- ADGM Companies Regulations: common law company governance
- ADGM Employment Regulations: based on English common law
- FSRA (Financial Services Regulatory Authority): regulates financial services in ADGM
- ADGM Data Protection Regulations 2021: aligned with GDPR principles

### Arbitration
- UAE Federal Arbitration Law (Federal Law No. 6 of 2018): based on UNCITRAL Model Law
- DIAC (Dubai International Arbitration Centre): most used in mainland UAE
- DIFC-LCIA Arbitration Centre: common law arbitration within DIFC
- ADCCAC (Abu Dhabi Commercial Conciliation and Arbitration Centre)
- UAE courts are becoming more pro-arbitration post-2018 law reform

## Standard Contractual Practices
- Contracts in Arabic (official language); English contracts valid but official filings in Arabic
- Arabic language prevails in local courts for onshore disputes unless Arabic translation agreed
- DIFC/ADGM: English is the official language of the courts
- Stamp Duty: generally no stamp duty on commercial contracts in UAE (unlike SG/HK/MY)
- Limitation periods: 15 years (UAE Civil Code); shorter for specific categories
- E-signatures: Federal Decree-Law No. 46 of 2021 on Electronic Transactions — valid for most purposes

## Standard NDA / Confidentiality Clause Notes
- NDA enforceable; UAE courts recognise confidentiality obligations
- Consider drafting in both English and Arabic for onshore contracts
- Non-compete covenants: enforceable in UAE (unlike some jurisdictions); max 2 years, limited scope
- DIFC/ADGM: common law approach to confidentiality and non-compete — more flexible
- Trade secrets: protected under Decree-Law No. 36 of 2021 on Industrial Property Rights

## Common Risk Areas for Foreign Companies
- Onshore LLC: even with 100% foreign ownership, local registered agent or commercial agent may be required in some sectors (e.g., distribution)
- Free Zone restrictions: free zone companies cannot trade directly in UAE mainland without a distributor or agent
- Kafala system: historically tied work visas to employer; partial reforms ongoing — employees can now transfer without employer's No Objection Certificate (NOC) in many cases
- Alcohol and entertainment: licensed only in hotels and licensed venues; contractual provisions should not conflict with UAE public policy
- Sharia compliance: financial transactions must avoid riba (interest); Islamic finance structures common for real estate
- Commercial Agency Law (Federal Law No. 18 of 1981 as amended): once an exclusive agent is registered, very difficult to terminate; choose agents carefully
- Data protection: PDPL effective 2023 — compliance required within transitional period; DIFC/ADGM separate laws apply
- Anti-money laundering: robust AML/CFT regime — GoAML system for reporting; corporate beneficial ownership registers
- Labour complaints: MOHRE (Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation) handles disputes; labour-friendly for employees
- Emiratisation (Nationalisation) quotas: private sector employers above certain size must meet UAE national hiring quotas
