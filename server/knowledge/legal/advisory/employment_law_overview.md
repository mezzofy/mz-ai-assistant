# Employment Law Overview — Asia-Pacific & Middle East

## Summary Comparison

| Feature | Singapore | Hong Kong | Malaysia | UAE | Saudi Arabia |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary Law** | Employment Act (Cap. 91) | Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57) | Employment Act 1955 | Labour Law (Federal Law 33/2021) | Labour Law (M/51) |
| **Min Notice** | 1 day – 4 weeks (by tenure) | 1 month | 4–8 weeks | 30 days | 60 days |
| **Severance** | No statutory minimum (MOM guidelines) | 2/3 × wage × years (>24 months) | Industrial Court discretion | End-of-service gratuity | End-of-service gratuity |
| **End-of-service gratuity** | N/A (CPF system) | N/A (MPF system) | N/A (EPF system) | 21 days/yr (first 5 yrs); 30 days thereafter | 1/2 month/yr (first 5); 1 month thereafter |
| **Social security** | CPF (20% employer + 17% employee — up to salary ceiling) | MPF (5% each — max HKD 1,500/month) | EPF (12% employer + 11% employee) + SOCSO + EIS | GPSSA (nationals only); private pensions for expatriates | GOSI (9.75% employer + 9.75% employee for Saudis) |
| **Maternity leave** | 16 weeks | 14 weeks | 98 days | 60 days | 10 weeks |
| **Non-compete** | Enforceable if reasonable | Enforceable if reasonable (strict test) | Void unless qualifying as trade secret protection | Enforceable; max 2 years | Enforceable; reasonable restrictions |
| **Dispute forum** | MOM / Employment Claims Tribunals | Labour Tribunal / District Court | Industrial Court | MOHRE / Labour Court | Labour Court |
| **Nationalisation** | FCF (Fair Consideration Framework) | No quota | No general quota | Emiratisation (Nitaqat) | Saudisation (Nitaqat) |

## Key Employment Issues by Jurisdiction

### Singapore
- CPF mandatory for SG citizens and PRs; non-compliance = criminal offence
- Employment Pass minimum salary: SGD 5,000/month (tech sector: SGD 5,500) — verify current thresholds
- Fair Consideration Framework: must advertise on MyCareersFuture.sg for 14 days before EP application
- Retrenchment: MOM notification required if ≥5 employees retrenched in 6 months (company ≥10 employees)

### Hong Kong
- MPF: 5% employer + 5% employee mandatory
- Statutory holiday pay: 12 days/year fully paid
- No at-will employment — Employment Ordinance provides implied continuous contract protections
- Wrongful dismissal: Labour Tribunal handles claims; quick and employee-friendly

### Malaysia
- Industrial Court: hearings are mandatory for unfair dismissal (no cap on awards)
- Minimum wage: MYR 1,500/month (peninsular Malaysia) — verify current rate
- Foreign workers: Employment Pass or Professional Visit Pass required; quota-based
- Trade unions: Industrial Relations Act governs; non-executive staff may unionise

### UAE
- All employees (expat and national) covered by Labour Law No. 33/2021
- Kafala reform: employees can transfer jobs without NOC; exit permits abolished
- Fixed-term contracts: maximum 3 years; automatically renewable
- Gratuity: non-waivable; mandatory payment on all departures except resignation <1 year
- DIFC Employment Law: more comprehensive; applies only within DIFC

### Saudi Arabia
- Saudisation (Nitaqat): enterprises must meet Saudi national quotas — affects visa eligibility
- Labor Law: contracts in Arabic mandatory; bilingual acceptable
- GOSI: mandatory for Saudi nationals and GCC nationals (expatriates exempt from Saudi GOSI)
- Probation: max 90 days (extendable to 180 by mutual agreement)
- Female employment: expanded dramatically under Vision 2030; driving, entertainment, sport sectors opened
