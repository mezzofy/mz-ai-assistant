# Regulatory Compliance Overview — Mezzofy Operating Jurisdictions

## Overview
This guide provides Leo with a high-level regulatory compliance framework for Mezzofy's
key operating jurisdictions. Focus: company registration, ongoing compliance, and key risks.

## Singapore

### Annual Compliance Requirements (Pte Ltd)
- Annual Return: file with ACRA within 5 months of financial year end (private companies)
- Financial Statements: prepare and file (audit required for companies ≥ SGD 10M revenue or ≥ 50 employees)
- AGM: small private companies exempt; others within 4 months of FYE
- GST: quarterly filing if registered (mandatory if turnover > SGD 1M)
- Corporate Tax: 17% headline rate; numerous exemptions for startups; ECI return due by 3 months after FYE
- Transfer Pricing: TP documentation required for related-party transactions above threshold
- MAS: Payment Services Act licence required for payment/e-money/digital token services

### Key Compliance Risks
- Late ACRA filings: fines up to SGD 5,000; striking off for persistent default
- Employment Pass quota: insufficient EP quota = inability to hire key foreign talent
- PDPA violations: up to SGD 1M fine or 10% annual SG turnover

## Hong Kong

### Annual Compliance Requirements (Private Ltd)
- Annual Return: file with CR within 42 days of anniversary of incorporation
- Financial Statements: prepare annually (audit required for all except dormant companies)
- Profits Tax: 16.5% (8.25% on first HKD 2M for single-entity); file HKIRD return by filing due date
- Business Registration: renew with IRAS annually (BRC renewal)
- MPF: monthly employer/employee contributions
- VAT: None in Hong Kong

### Key Compliance Risks
- CR filings: significant director/shareholder change — notify CR within 15 days
- SFC licensing: failure to have correct SFC licence for regulated activities = criminal offence
- AML compliance: FI compliance with AMLO (Anti-Money Laundering Ordinance) mandatory

## Malaysia

### Annual Compliance Requirements (Sdn Bhd)
- Annual Return: file with SSM within 30 days of AGM or within 6 months of financial year end
- Financial Statements: audit required for most Sdn Bhd companies
- Income Tax: 24% corporate tax (17% for SMEs on first MYR 600K); LHDN filing by 7 months after FYE
- SST: SST (Sales and Service Tax) at 6–10%; registration mandatory if taxable turnover > MYR 500K
- EPF/SOCSO/EIS: monthly employer contribution obligations
- Annual General Meeting: within 6 months of financial year end

### Key Compliance Risks
- SSM: late filings attract fines; directors personally liable for compliance
- PDPA: personal data breaches; inadequate data protection policies
- Labor court exposure: unfair dismissal claims common; Industrial Court awards can be significant

## UAE

### Annual Compliance Requirements
- Trade licence renewal: annual renewal with DED (mainland) or free zone authority
- Financial statements: audit required for many structures; submit to authority
- Corporate tax: 9% on taxable income > AED 375,000 (effective June 2023) — significant change
- VAT: 5% VAT; mandatory registration if revenue > AED 375,000; quarterly filing
- Economic substance: certain activities require substance reports annually
- AML/CFT: compliance programme mandatory; submit annual report to relevant authority

### Key Compliance Risks
- Trade licence lapse: criminal liability for operating without valid licence
- Corporate tax: new regime (from 2023); transfer pricing rules apply
- Emiratisation: fines for non-compliance with national hiring quotas (Nafis system)

## Saudi Arabia

### Annual Compliance Requirements
- MISA: annual renewal of investment licence
- Ministry of Commerce: annual renewal of commercial registration
- Zakat/Income Tax: Saudi entities pay Zakat (2.5% of net worth); foreign entities pay 20% income tax
- VAT: 15% VAT; mandatory filing for registered businesses; quarterly or monthly depending on revenue
- Saudisation (Nitaqat): quarterly compliance reporting; visa quota linked to compliance level
- GOSI: monthly social insurance contributions for Saudi/GCC national employees

### Key Compliance Risks
- Nitaqat non-compliance: loss of government contract eligibility; visa restrictions
- Labour Law: active enforcement; Labour Courts employee-friendly
- PDPL: new data protection law — compliance deadline has passed; enforcement increasing

## Key Cross-Jurisdictional Compliance Principles
1. Maintain a compliance calendar for each jurisdiction
2. Engage local registered agents in each jurisdiction for filing obligations
3. Annual review of regulatory requirements — laws change frequently in GCC
4. Transfer pricing: apply arm's-length pricing for all inter-company transactions
5. AML/KYC: mandatory in all jurisdictions; Mezzofy must screen counterparties in regulated activities
