# International Business Law — Overview for Mezzofy Operations

## Overview
Mezzofy operates across Asia-Pacific and the Middle East. This guide provides Leo with
key international business law principles for the jurisdictions we operate in.

## Key Principles for Cross-Border Contracts

### Choice of Law and Forum
1. Always specify governing law AND jurisdiction/arbitration seat in every commercial contract.
2. Arbitration is strongly preferred for cross-border contracts (160+ countries enforce under New York Convention).
3. Best neutral venues for APAC: Singapore (SIAC), Hong Kong (HKIAC).
4. Best neutral venues for Middle East: DIFC-LCIA/DIAC (UAE), SCCA (Saudi Arabia).

### Cross-Border Enforcement
- New York Convention countries: SG, HK, MY, UAE, KSA, QA, Cayman — all enforce foreign arbitral awards.
- Court judgments: enforcement between common law jurisdictions (SG, HK, Cayman) generally straightforward.
- UAE/Saudi/Qatar court judgments: enforceable in GCC under Riyadh Arab Agreement on Judicial Cooperation.

## International Contract Formation

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Language:** In UAE and Saudi Arabia, Arabic language requirements apply for official purposes.
2. **Notarisation:** Some countries require notarised or legalised documents (apostille) for foreign enforcement.
3. **Signature authority:** Verify signatory authority — check corporate resolutions, articles, POA.
4. **Currency risk:** Specify currency; include provisions for FX fluctuation in long-term contracts.
5. **CISG:** United Nations Convention on International Sale of Goods (CISG) may apply automatically to cross-border sale of goods contracts unless expressly excluded.

## Regulatory Compliance for International Operations

### Anti-Corruption (Multi-Jurisdiction)
- Singapore: Prevention of Corruption Act (PCA) — corporate liability
- HK: Prevention of Bribery Ordinance (POBO)
- Malaysia: MACC Act 2009 — commercial organisations liable for bribery by associated persons
- UAE: Federal Decree-Law No. 31 of 2021 (Crimes Law) — anti-bribery provisions
- Saudi Arabia: NAZAHA enforcement — active anti-corruption regime
- International: UK Bribery Act 2010 applies to UK-connected companies worldwide; FCPA (US) for US-listed companies

### Sanctions
- Check OFAC (US), EU, UK, UN sanctions lists before engaging counterparties.
- UAE/Saudi/Qatar: local sanctions regimes; UN sanctions applied domestically.
- Always conduct KYC/AML checks on counterparties in high-risk sectors.

## Key Takeaways for Leo
- Jurisdiction selection is one of the most important decisions in international contracting.
- Singapore: gold standard for Asia commercial contracts — strong rule of law, English language, SIAC.
- Cayman Islands: gold standard for fund structures — no tax, flexibility, global recognition.
- UAE/DIFC: best for Middle East regional HQ and financial services.
- Always build in: governing law, dispute resolution, force majeure, data protection, IP ownership, and limitation of liability.
