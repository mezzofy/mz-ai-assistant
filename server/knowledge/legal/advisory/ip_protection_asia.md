# IP Protection in Asia-Pacific and Middle East

## Overview
Intellectual property protection varies significantly across jurisdictions. This guide helps Leo advise on IP registration strategy, enforcement, and contractual IP clauses.

## Jurisdiction-by-Jurisdiction Summary

### Singapore
- **Trade Marks:** Register with IPOS; standard registration takes 9–12 months; 10-year renewal.
- **Patents:** IPOS examination; 20-year protection; examination takes 3–5 years.
- **Copyright:** Automatic protection on creation (Copyright Act 2021); no registration required; life + 70 years.
- **Trade Secrets:** Protected through contractual NDA and common law breach of confidence.
- **Enforcement:** Singapore IP Court — efficient; interim injunctions readily granted.
- **IP Holding Incentive:** IP Development Incentive (IDI) — reduced tax rate on qualifying IP income.

### Hong Kong
- **Trade Marks:** IPD (Intellectual Property Department); registration ~9–12 months; 10-year renewal.
- **Patents:** Short-term patent (re-registration of GB/EP/CN patents); standard patent (substantive examination); 20-year protection.
- **Copyright:** Automatic; life + 50 years (literary works) — shorter than Singapore.
- **Trade Secrets:** Equitable breach of confidence; employer owns employee's work-for-hire.
- **Enforcement:** IP Court within District Court; HKSAR courts experienced in IP disputes.
- **Note:** Chinese copyright may overlap for PRC-originating works — dual protection strategy recommended.

### Malaysia
- **Trade Marks:** MyIPO (Malaysia IP Corporation); standard registration; Madrid Protocol accession 2019.
- **Patents:** MyIPO examination; 20-year protection.
- **Copyright:** Copyright Act 1987; automatic; life + 50 years.
- **Industrial Designs:** ID Act 1996; 5-year registration (extendable to 25 years).
- **Enforcement:** IP Court (Kuala Lumpur); criminal enforcement available for copyright.

### UAE
- **Trade Marks:** MOCCAE (Ministry of Climate Change and Environment handles TM in UAE mainland); free zone TMs registered separately.
- **Patents:** UAE Patent Office; examination-based; GCC Patent Office also available.
- **Copyright:** Federal Law No. 38 of 2021; life + 50 years; moral rights protected.
- **Trade Secrets:** Federal IP law (Decree-Law No. 36 of 2021) specifically protects undisclosed information/trade secrets.
- **DIFC IP:** Common law approach; employer ownership of employee IP by contract.
- **Enforcement:** UAE courts; DIFC Courts for DIFC entities; piracy enforcement active.

### Saudi Arabia
- **Trade Marks:** SAIP (Saudi Authority for Intellectual Property) — rapid modernisation under Vision 2030.
- **Patents:** SAIP examination; 20-year protection.
- **Copyright:** Life + 70 years (one of the longest in the region).
- **Trade Secrets:** SAIP Trade Secret Regulations (2020) — specific protection.
- **Enforcement:** SAIP enforcement directorate; criminal sanctions for infringement.
- **Note:** AI-generated works: unclear ownership under current law — legal counsel recommended.

### Cayman Islands
- **Trade Marks:** Register in Cayman (limited local market) or rely on UK/EU/US/SG marks for commercial protection.
- **No local IP court:** Common law principles apply; English precedents followed.
- **IP Holding Structures:** Cayman exempted company used as IP holding vehicle — zero tax on royalty income (but economic substance rules may apply to IP holding activities).
- **Licensing:** Royalty streams from Cayman IP holding company to operating entities — widely used but BEPS/economic substance rules now apply.

## IP Registration Strategy for Mezzofy

### Priority Markets
1. Singapore — strong base; IPOS registration recommended for all Mezzofy marks and core technology
2. Hong Kong — critical for Greater China presence; register marks at IPD
3. Malaysia — growing market; MyIPO registration recommended
4. UAE — Middle East hub; MOCCAE and DIFC registration as appropriate
5. International — Madrid Protocol for cost-efficient multi-country registration

### Key Contract IP Clauses
- All technology partnerships: clear IP ownership clause (who owns what created during the engagement)
- Employment contracts: IP assignment clause to ensure employee inventions vest in employer
- Vendor agreements: work-for-hire clause to ensure deliverables vest in Mezzofy
- Licensing: specify territory, exclusivity, royalty, and sub-licensing rights clearly
